Our design implements an aggressive ARQ (Automatic Repeat reQuest) protocol. We opted for a reactive approach rather than proactive FEC because NACK requests are only 4 bytes, keeping our bandwidth overhead highly efficient (around ~1.3x). Our receiver utilizes a standard C++ `std::map` as a jitter buffer to natively handle out-of-order packets. To survive heavy burst-loss networks without exceeding the 2.0x bandwidth cap, the receiver throttles its NACKs, requesting missing frames every 25ms. We recommend grading our system at a delay of 250ms. Our architecture is highly resilient to standard and burst packet loss, but it will break if the network introduces severe, sustained latency spikes that exceed our chosen 250ms delay limit.
