Our design implements an aggressive ARQ (Automatic Repeat reQuest) protocol. We opted for a reactive approach rather than proactive FEC because NACK requests are only 4 bytes, allowing us to maintain a ~1.2x bandwidth overhead while comfortably staying under the 2.0x limit. Our receiver utilizes a standard C++ `std::map` as a jitter buffer to natively handle out-of-order packets and automatically blasts NACKs every 5ms for any sequence gaps between our expected packet and the highest received sequence. We recommend grading our system at a delay of 100ms. Our architecture is highly resilient to standard packet loss, but it will break if the network introduces severe, sustained latency spikes that exceed our chosen delay limit, as the Round Trip Time for our NACKs will push the recovered packets past the strict harness deadlines.
